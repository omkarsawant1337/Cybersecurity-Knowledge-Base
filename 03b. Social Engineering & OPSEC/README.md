## 📂 Topics Covered

### 🎭 Social Engineering Attacks

Understand how attackers manipulate people rather than technology to gain unauthorized access.

Topics include:

- What is Social Engineering? Attack Lifecycle & Human Psychology
- Reconnaissance, Victim Selection, Relationship Building, Exploitation
- Human-Based, Computer-Based & Mobile-Based Attacks
- Phishing, Spear Phishing, Whaling, Smishing, Vishing
- Shoulder Surfing, Dumpster Diving, Tailgating, Piggybacking
- Identity Theft & Insider Threats
- Security Awareness & Prevention Best Practices

### 🎯 Social Engineering Toolkit (SET)

Learn one of the most popular frameworks for authorized social engineering assessments.

Topics include:

- Introduction to SET & Installation on Kali Linux
- Spear Phishing, Website Cloner, Credential Harvester
- Payload Generation & USB/HID Attack Simulation
- QR Code Generator, Web Templates, Mass Mailer
- PowerShell Modules & Typical Workflow
- Security Awareness Exercises & Authorized Red Team Assessments

### 📧 Phishing Campaign Infrastructure

Understand how organizations conduct phishing awareness campaigns safely and ethically.

Topics include:

- GoPhish — Email Templates, SMTP Servers, Landing Pages
- Campaign Management & User Groups
- Awareness Metrics & Tracking Metrics
- SPF, DKIM, DMARC & Email Authentication
- Campaign Workflow, Defensive Best Practices, Compliance Reporting

### 🕵 Anonymity, VPNs & ProxyChains

Learn the technologies used to enhance privacy and protect network traffic.

Topics include:

- VPN Fundamentals — Encryption, Protocols, Types, Remote Access
- Public Wi-Fi Protection, Kill Switch, No-Log Policies
- Proxy Servers — SOCKS, HTTP, SSL
- ProxyChains — Dynamic Chains, Strict Chains, Tor Integration
- Anonymous Browsing & Privacy Best Practices

### 🧅 Tails OS & Whonix

Explore privacy-focused operating systems designed for anonymity and secure research.

Topics include:

- Tails OS & Whonix — Live and Amnesic Operating Systems
- Tor Network Fundamentals
- Gateway & Workstation Architecture (Whonix)
- Persistent Storage & Virtual Machines
- Operational Security (OPSEC) & Secure Browsing
- Privacy Applications & Anonymous Communication
- Secure Research Environments
- Tails vs. Whonix Comparison

### 🔐 Secure Communications for Red Teams

Learn how professional security teams protect sensitive information during engagements.

Topics include:

- Secure Communication Workflow
- GPG, OpenPGP, PGP Encryption
- Public Key Cryptography & Private Keys
- Digital Signatures & File/Email Encryption
- Signal Messenger & End-to-End Encryption
- Secure File Sharing & Client Confidentiality
- Secure Report Delivery & Best Practices

## 🚀 Why Learn These Topics?

Human-focused attacks remain one of the most effective methods used by attackers. Understanding social engineering, phishing infrastructure, operational security, and secure communications helps security professionals assess organizations, improve user awareness, and protect sensitive information during authorized engagements.

By mastering these topics, you'll be able to:

- Recognize social engineering techniques
- Build and understand phishing awareness campaigns
- Improve email security knowledge, including SPF, DKIM, and DMARC
- Enhance online privacy using VPNs and Tor
- Configure ProxyChains for privacy-focused environments
- Compare Tails and Whonix use cases
- Encrypt sensitive files and emails with GPG/PGP
- Use secure communication tools during assessments
- Strengthen operational security (OPSEC) practices
